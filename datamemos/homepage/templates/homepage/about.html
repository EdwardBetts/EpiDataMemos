About

This website is designed to improve the discussion around HIV in San Francisco by presenting the information online, and allow for analysis and user generated analysis to be saved in context of the information. The idea is that if epidemiology information is easier to cite, it will be better understood and used. This prototype was made for an audience of health professionals.

The charts on this website are simply presentation layer for statistics about HIV in San Francisco. The information that is presented here was compiled by epidemiologists in the HIV Surveillance Section of the San Francisco Department of Public Health.

How to Use

Open a chart from the list. Navigate the chart by selecting a category. You can hover or click on any bar to view details.

As you navigate the chart, notes will organize themselves to show you the most relevant information.

To see what a note references, hover or click on the note's bookmark.

To add a comment to the chart, go to 'comments' and click 'Add Note'. You are required to enter your email address to post on a chart. After posting a note, you will be sent an email with a permanent link to your note, and a link to edit that note.

When you share a note, you will copy the permanent link shown to you and paste it into

To share a note with out leaving a comment, you can click the share note button.
